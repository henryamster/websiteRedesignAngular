About Meexpand_more


I grew up in Richmond, Kentucky, near the historic Fort Boonesboro State Park and attended Madison Central High School, where I was an officer in the Junior Classical League (Latin Club), participated in the Academic Team all 4 years and was a participant in the National Youth Leadership Foundation Conference for Technology in San Diego in 2006.

After graduating high school, I moved to Lexington, Kentucky, to attend the University of Kentucky, which I did on and off over the next five years. I was a volunteer DJ at UK's college radio station, WRFL, during this time.

Over the following decade, I tried to make the most of my twenties: I moved to Louisville to start a band, I worked a number of gigs as a FoH sound engineer, graphic designer, and web developer, and ended up going to Eastern Kentucky University to finish a degree in Computer Information Systems. For the majority of this time I worked full-time with Meijer, in a number of different positions in the retail environment. I spent a couple years in management and after completing my degree, took an internship on the Infrastructure team at Meijer's corporate headquarters in Grand Rapids, Michigan. I ended up turning down a position there so that I could stay close to home, and wound up at my current job at EKU Training Resource Center, where I've been working since 2019.

In my time outside of work I enjoy playing, writing/arranging, and recording music, making 3d motion graphics, and playing with generative art. I watch every UK Basketball game, my artwork was recently featured in The Goldenrod, and (like everyone else) I'm always listening to music or a podcast.
